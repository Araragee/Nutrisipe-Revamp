# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Home cooks — everyday people browsing for meal inspiration, saving recipes they want to try, and occasionally sharing their own. They open Nutrisipe casually, often from their phone, in a relaxed context (evening scrolling, weekend meal planning). Their primary job: find something good to cook tonight, or save it for later. They are not nutritionists or influencers; they cook for enjoyment and for people they care about.

## Product Purpose

Nutrisipe is a social recipe-sharing app. It gives home cooks a feed of recipe ideas to browse, save, and recreate, with the ability to follow creators whose food resonates with them. Secondary surfaces — ingredient exploration, recipe creation, real-time messaging — deepen engagement for users who want to go further. Success looks like a user opening the app when they don't know what to cook and leaving with a plan.

## Brand Personality

Warm, social, playful. Nutrisipe feels like a friend who loves food and wants to share it with you — never a nutritionist lecturing you, never a chef performing for status. The tone is encouraging and generous. Food here is pleasure, not optimization.

## Anti-references

- **Overcrowded food blogs**: no walls of text before the recipe, no ads, no life-story preambles. Content is immediate and scannable.
- **Corporate SaaS dashboards**: not enterprise-blue, not form-heavy, not dense. No data-table thinking applied to recipes.

## Design Principles

1. **Food first.** Recipe imagery is the primary content. Every layout decision should serve the photos, not frame them.
2. **Delight in the scroll.** The feed should feel alive — varied card sizes, subtle motion, a sense that there's always something new below.
3. **Warmth over efficiency.** Where a choice exists between "faster" and "nicer to be in," choose nicer. This is a leisure app.
4. **Social without the noise.** Follower counts and likes are present but never the hero. The recipe, not the metrics, takes center stage.
5. **Approachable complexity.** Ingredient tools and nutrition data exist, but never intrude on users who didn't ask for them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Sufficient contrast for all text on food photography backgrounds (overlay treatment required). Keyboard-navigable feed and modals. Reduced-motion respect via `prefers-reduced-motion`. No color-only information encoding.
